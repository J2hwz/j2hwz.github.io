I am a fourth-year undergraduate student reading Philosophy and Psychology at the University of Edinburgh. While I am still exploring various topics within these fields, I am broadly interested in how humans communicate (or fail to do so), both in physical and online settings. Additionally, I am keen to learn about scientific communication, counterfactual reasoning, and philosophical logic. I've utilised both text mining and online surveys to study questions in these areas.

My third-year undergraduate project supervised by Zachary Horne has just been published in the CogSci 2024 proceedings, where I investigated the linguistic and topical markers that are associated with sarcasm on Reddit.

This Summer, I am working with Zachary once again to investigate the factors that influence our judgements of consensus given different evidence, where a key aim is to improve how we communicate scientific findings on pressing issues such as climate change. This project is funded by a Carnegie Vacation Scholarship and the Our Minds Programme.

Outside of my studies, I enjoy bouldering, travelling, and Mary's Milk Bar (best ice cream in Edinburgh).

This website is very much a work in progress. Feedback is very welcome :)
